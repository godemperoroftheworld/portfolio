title: I Know Better
description: An interior monologue I wrote to be performed in my theatre and performance course at the University of Waterloo.

Death isn’t something I’ve found myself wishing for. Not historically. I never understood it. The desire to end. Today, I understand. Not really, not in a tangible way, not with the pain and suffering. But here, in the present, wishing these moments just weren’t. Wishing that I could cease being a being, if only just for now.

I stare into the emptiness in front of me, unaware of the 20-some students encircling me. Why must I be? Is it really death if life doesn’t feel like I’m alive? I look at the lines on my hands, if they are my hands. All I see are creases upon creases, lines crossing out the hundreds of mistakes I have made, and the hundreds of mistakes yet to come. Scars. The particularly bad ones come out as scars. Have I really made that many?

I continue to stare, blankly at the empty space in front of this withered palm. Is that all age is? Marks of a life not lived, a series of mistakes, one blunder flowing into the next. Each bringing me closer to the end. Every time I blink, more and more mistakes pass me by. Would it really be that bad to just skip all this, to the end? Would it really do harm? To sleep an endless slumber?

At least in my dreams there’s beauty. They say a life without beauty is not worth living, but I’d question if it was really a life at all. I used to think it was, when I didn’t understand. That it was about the journey, that one day, I’d find it. Now, I’m starting to think I know better.
