title: 'Breaking the Script: Trans Aesthetics and Creating “Something Else To Be”'
description: A film program essay on "I Saw the TV Glow", "The Matrix" and "Love Lies Bleeding"
content: Under contemporary regimes of control, heteropatriarchal scripts remain a pervasive, colonizing force– a “product and producer of carceral logics” that seek to relegate us into conformity, and a drive to “assimilation” that is “killing us”  (Bey 137; Cohen 445). By concealing “labour under the sign of nature”, these directives masquerade as absolute truths, concealing a precarious construction that requires constant enforcement (Ahmed 145). For the survival of queer subjects, rupturing from these norms is vital, propelling us toward “another way of being”– one that generates new “life narratives” and alternative queer “relations to time and space” (Malatino 124; Halberstam 13). This ideological struggle is viscerally mirrored in the language of modern cinema, where Hollywood’s rigid continuity and genre convention enforce a manufactured “normal”. Functioning as an aesthetic extension of the “petro-sexo-racial capitalist regime”, this “narrative construction of heteronormativity” falsely purports itself as “pure reality” (Preciado 19,37; Keegan 136). The films in this program violently reject this facade, employing distinct visual and structural dysfluency to break from dominant norms. Through their trans-gressive formal properties, _I Saw the TV Glow_, _The Matrix_, and _Love Lies Bleeding_ make visible the conventional script and its damage, breaking with it to embrace the “livable wreckage” and imagine “something else to be” (Bey 146; Fawaz 335).
